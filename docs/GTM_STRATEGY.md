# PlotDNA GTM Strategy

## Executive Summary

PlotDNA should go to market as a buyer-side investment screening product, not as a generic real estate app or listing portal. The strongest wedge is helping serious land and property buyers understand whether a micro-market is worth deeper due diligence before they talk to brokers, visit sites, or commit capital.

The first launch should be Hyderabad-first. Hyderabad has enough existing product coverage, strong land-investment demand, and clear micro-market narratives around Adibatla, Tukkuguda, Kokapet, Narsingi, Mokila, Shankarpally, Shamshabad, and other corridors.

The Android app should not be the first growth channel. It should come after web users show repeated usage, report sharing, and paid intent.

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> PlotDNA is the investment due-diligence layer for Indian real estate buyers.

Short version:

> Screen the location before you trust the pitch.

What PlotDNA is:
- A buyer-side screening and decision-support product.
- A micro-market intelligence layer.
- A report engine for location score, risk, upside, and verification checklist.
- A way to compare areas before site visits.

What PlotDNA is not:
- Not a listing portal.
- Not a broker marketplace.
- Not legal advice.
- Not a guaranteed valuation tool.
- Not a replacement for title, RERA, zoning, or encumbrance verification.

## First Customer Segment

### Primary Segment

NRI and out-of-city buyers evaluating Hyderabad plots or apartments.

Why they are attractive:
- High ticket size.
- High anxiety around fraud and misinformation.
- Low ability to physically verify every location.
- Strong need to explain decisions to family or advisors.
- Willingness to pay for clarity if trust is high.

Core user problem:

> "I am seeing broker/developer claims about a location, but I do not know if the area is actually investable or just hype."

### Secondary Segment

Small brokers, real estate advisors, and channel partners who need polished reports to educate clients.

Why they are useful:
- They already have distribution.
- They can share reports through WhatsApp.
- They can become repeat users if reports help them close conversations.

Risk:
- If broker-led growth dominates too early, PlotDNA may lose buyer-trust positioning. Keep buyer trust as the core brand promise.

## Buyer Jobs To Be Done

1. "Help me know if this area is worth visiting."
2. "Help me compare two or three areas before I decide."
3. "Help me identify what can go wrong legally or structurally."
4. "Help me challenge a broker's claim with data."
5. "Help me explain the decision to family or partners."
6. "Help me avoid paying a hype premium."

## Product Wedge

The wedge is not "search property listings." The wedge is:

> Location and micro-market due diligence before property selection.

First paid product:
- Area investment report.
- Compare-3-areas report.
- Custom location due-diligence request.

The report should answer:
- Is this area Buy, Wait, Avoid, or Investigate?
- What is the best use case: end-use, rental, 3-year investment, 7-year land bank, or speculative?
- What is the main upside?
- What is the main risk?
- What price range looks reasonable?
- What must be verified before paying an advance?

## Funnel

### Stage 1: Awareness

Channels:
- SEO pages for micro-markets.
- LinkedIn posts.
- Reddit/Quora answers where allowed.
- WhatsApp sharing through brokers and investor groups.
- YouTube/short-form comparison content.

Content examples:
- "Adibatla vs Tukkuguda: which is better for 5-year land investment?"
- "Kokapet vs Narsingi: premium growth or overpricing risk?"
- "Mokila vs Shankarpally: villa corridor or long-horizon land bank?"
- "Hyderabad plot buying checklist before paying token advance."
- "How to verify HMDA/DTCP/RERA before buying land."

### Stage 2: Activation

User lands on:
- Hyderabad Land Investment Scanner.
- Area detail page.
- Comparison page.

Activation action:
- Search an area or coordinate.
- View investment verdict.
- Compare two or three areas.

### Stage 3: Lead Capture

Trigger lead capture after value is shown:
- Unlock full report.
- Download PDF.
- Compare 3 areas.
- Request custom due-diligence report.

Fields:
- Name.
- Email or phone.
- Buyer type: NRI, local buyer, broker/advisor, developer/channel partner.
- Area of interest.
- Budget range.
- Timeline: immediate, 3 months, 6 months, research only.

### Stage 4: Monetization

Start with one-time purchases:
- Rs 99 to Rs 199: single detailed area report.
- Rs 499 to Rs 999: compare 3 to 5 areas with PDF.
- Rs 2,999 to Rs 9,999: custom due-diligence pack for a specific location.

Add subscriptions only after repeat usage is proven:
- Broker/advisor tier: Rs 1,999 to Rs 4,999/month.
- Branded reports.
- Saved client comparisons.
- Report export history.

### Stage 5: Retention

Retention hooks:
- Saved watchlist.
- Monthly area signal update.
- Price/risk movement alerts.
- "Your watched area changed" email.
- New RERA/project activity alerts where reliable.

## 30-Day Launch Plan

### Week 1: Trust Foundation

Ship:
- Hyderabad investment scanner positioning.
- Area page investment verdict.
- Data basis and confidence labels.
- Buyer due-diligence checklist.
- Report/download CTA.

Success metric:
- Users understand what the product does within 10 seconds.

### Week 2: Comparison Workflow

Ship:
- Compare 3 Hyderabad areas.
- Shareable comparison summary.
- Lead capture for full report.

Success metric:
- At least 20 percent of activated users try comparison.

### Week 3: Content Distribution

Publish:
- 10 micro-market pages or posts.
- 5 comparison posts.
- 3 buyer checklist posts.
- 1 launch page optimized for "Hyderabad land investment scanner".

Success metric:
- 500 unique visitors.
- 50 qualified leads.

### Week 4: Paid Intent Test

Ship:
- Report request workflow.
- Manual payment or early payment link.
- Custom due-diligence request form.

Success metric:
- 10 custom report requests.
- 5 paid-intent conversations.
- 3 paid reports or clear willingness-to-pay signals.

## 90-Day Plan

### Month 1: Validate Demand

Goal:
- Prove users want area screening and comparison.

Targets:
- 500 to 1,000 visitors.
- 50 to 100 leads.
- 10 to 20 report requests.
- 3 to 5 paid reports or paid pilots.

### Month 2: Validate Monetization

Goal:
- Prove users will pay for reports.

Targets:
- 20 paid reports.
- 3 broker/advisor pilots.
- 5 repeat users or referrals.
- First user testimonials.

### Month 3: Validate Repeatability

Goal:
- Prove one acquisition channel can repeat.

Targets:
- 3 SEO/content pages producing steady traffic.
- 2 broker/advisor partners actively sharing reports.
- 50 paid-report attempts or qualified custom-report requests.
- Decide whether to add Android, payments, or B2B dashboard next.

## Channel Strategy

### SEO

Best long-term channel. Build pages around:
- Area investment guide.
- Area vs area comparison.
- Land buying checklist.
- RERA/HMDA/DTCP verification guide.
- Corridor guide.

### LinkedIn

Use founder-led posts:
- Short area comparisons.
- "What brokers say vs what data shows."
- Buyer checklist threads.
- Before/after report examples.

### WhatsApp

Most important sharing loop.

Reports should be:
- Mobile-readable.
- PDF-friendly.
- Easy to forward.
- Branded but not bloated.

### YouTube/Shorts

Good for trust, but slower to execute. Start with simple screen-recorded explainers:
- "3 risks in buying near Adibatla."
- "How to compare Kokapet and Narsingi."
- "Do not buy a plot before checking these documents."

### Paid Ads

Avoid heavy paid ads at first. Use small tests only after landing page conversion is known.

## Release Gates Before Android

Do not prioritize Android until:
- Web funnel captures leads.
- Users download/share reports.
- At least 20 paid reports or strong paid-intent requests exist.
- Repeat usage is visible.
- Users ask for saved watchlists or mobile convenience.

Android should focus on:
- Saved watchlist.
- Report access.
- Site-visit checklist.
- Alerts.

It should not be used as the first demand-generation channel.

## Risks And Mitigations

### Risk: Users do not trust scores

Mitigation:
- Show data basis, confidence, date, and checklist.
- Tie claims to source categories.
- Label estimates clearly.

### Risk: App launch gets no downloads

Mitigation:
- Launch web/content first.
- Use Android only after pull exists.

### Risk: Legal liability from investment language

Mitigation:
- Use screening language.
- Show disclaimers before report actions.
- Avoid guaranteed return claims.

### Risk: Broker channel weakens buyer trust

Mitigation:
- Keep buyer-side brand.
- Do not sell buyer leads in phase one.
- Offer broker reports without hiding buyer disclaimers.

### Risk: Paid acquisition is too expensive

Mitigation:
- Use SEO, comparison content, founder-led posts, and partnerships first.
- Test paid ads only after conversion is known.
